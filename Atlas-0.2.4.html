Added

  • A redesigned iPhone editor. Editing a note on iPhone now feels like a focused writing surface, in the spirit of Apple's Journal app: refined, slightly smaller typography with tighter line spacing, a thin divider under the note's title, and a clean floating top bar while you write (undo, formatting, done). Tap Aa and a Format panel slides up in place of the keyboard — bold, italic, strikethrough, highlight, inline code, headings, quotes, lists, indenting, and links, all in one place.
  • Quick actions while writing on iPhone. A floating bar at the bottom gives one-tap access to discovered connections, inserting an image from your photo library, and linking to another note.
  • Discovered connections on iPhone. Tap the spark button while writing to see the notes Atlas thinks are related, read why each one connects, and link it — the same intelligence the Mac and iPad show in the side panel.
  • Version history for every note. Atlas now keeps a private, on-device timeline of your notes as you write. Open Version History from the note menu to browse earlier versions, see exactly what changed against the previous one, and restore any of them with a tap — restoring just writes that text back as a new edit, so nothing is lost. History lives only on this device and is never uploaded; old versions are pruned automatically over time, and you can clear a single note's history or all of it from Settings.
  • Pull-over sidebar on iPhone. Swipe in from the left edge — or tap the menu button — to reveal the sidebar (Daily, All Notes, Regions, Pinned, Recent). The page slides aside to uncover it with a soft, rounded-card look, and the menu items fade and settle into place as it opens.
  • Sync across your devices. First-run setup now lets you choose where your notes live: keep them on this device, or put your vault in iCloud Drive to use the same notes on your Mac, iPad, and iPhone. On Mac, Atlas finds your iCloud Drive automatically; on iPad and iPhone you pick (or create) the folder.
  • What's New, in the app. The app menu's "What's New" now opens a built-in changelog so you can see what each update brought without leaving Atlas.
  • Help, in the app. "Help" opens a quick reference — keyboard shortcuts and a short guide to writing, linking, search, and the AI features — all offline.
  • Terms & Privacy, in the app. A clear, in-app write-up of how Atlas handles your notes: what works offline, and exactly what's sent to Voyage AI and OpenAI (and when) once you add your own keys. Atlas flags it when it changes.

Changed

  • More reliable undo & redo. Undo (⌘Z) and redo (⌘⇧Z) now step through your edits predictably and put the cursor back where it was when you made the change, so picking up after an undo feels natural.

Fixed

  • The cursor lands where you expect after Return. Pressing Return at the end of a line no longer leaves the cursor drawn on the wrong line until you start typing — fixed on Mac, iPad, and iPhone.
  • Inserted images fit the page. Images you add now scale to the width of the note instead of overflowing at their full size.
