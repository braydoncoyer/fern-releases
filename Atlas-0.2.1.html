Added

  • Tables. Write standard Markdown tables and Atlas renders them with aligned columns, a header rule, and column dividers. Tables stretch to fill the writing width, and on narrow screens they scale down to stay readable instead of clipping. Put the cursor inside a table to edit the raw Markdown; it snaps back to the formatted view when you move out.
  • Callouts. Start a quote line with > [!note], [!tip], [!warning], [!danger], or [!question] for a colored, titled callout block.
  • Footnotes. Reference notes with [^1] and define them with [^1]: …; the reference renders as a superscript link.
  • Share to Atlas (iPad): Share links, articles, or images to Atlas from any app. Each capture becomes a new note and a timestamped link appears under a Saved heading in today's daily note. A quick confirmation shows it landed; the note is yours to review and rename later. Captures are tagged #inbox by default — change the tag in Settings → Shared Items (or clear it for none).
  • macOS: The sidebar now has back/forward navigation and a new-note button in its header, matching the iPad. They live in the sidebar (not the editor) so a distraction-free writing view with all panels closed stays chrome-free.

Changed

  • Recents now lists notes you've visited, not just ones you've edited — opening a note moves it to the top, matching the convention in Craft and Bear.
  • iPad (narrow window): The sidebar toggle is now a clear sidebar icon instead of a chevron, so it's no longer confused with the back/forward navigation buttons.
  • Calmer, smarter related-note suggestions. Atlas now waits until you pause or move on from a note before working out its "related notes" in the background, instead of starting a second after you stop typing — so it stays out of the way while you're thinking. It focuses that work on the notes you actually open, and a brand-new note surfaces its first connections after a short pause, with no need to leave and come back.
  • Bolding, italicizing, or highlighting a word — or tidying up whitespace — no longer makes Atlas re-analyze the note.

Fixed

  • iPad: The info panel now closes automatically when you open a different note, instead of lingering over the new note's content.
  • iPad: The tag autocomplete list no longer pops open on its own when you open a note whose last line ends in a #tag (it now appears only while typing).
  • Scroll position stays put when toggling panels. On longer notes, opening or closing the sidebar or info panel no longer jumps you away from where you were reading.
  • Typing works immediately in an empty note. Opening an empty note no longer swallows the first line — you can type right away instead of pressing Return first.
  • Unformatting in one tap. Selecting bold, italic, or highlighted text and tapping the formatting button now removes the formatting, instead of sometimes applying it a second time.
