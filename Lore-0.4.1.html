Added

  • Rename a tag everywhere. Right-click any tag in the context panel and choose Rename Tag… to change it across your whole vault at once — every #tag in your notes and their frontmatter. Lore previews exactly how many notes will change before you commit, warns when the new name already exists (a merge), and keeps a Region's membership intact if its tag is the one you rename. Undo the whole cascade from the confirmation banner. Works on Mac, iPad, and iPhone.
  • A serif reading voice. Notes, titles, and the app's chrome are now set in New York — the system serif — for a warmer, more book-like page. Prefer the old look? Settings → Appearance → Font switches between Serif, Sans-Serif (Geist), and Mono (Geist Mono).
  • Insert an image or file from the / menu. Type / in a note and choose Image or File to pick files from disk — they're copied into your vault's Attachments folder and embedded right where your cursor was. Works on Mac and iPad.
  • Labs. A new Settings pane for early experiments, each switchable per-device and off by default.
  • Retelling (Labs, Mac). Now and then, Lore notices a thread of linked notes that has gone quiet and offers to walk you back through it — a short, focused re-read, one note at a time, ending with a single question whose answer becomes a new note linked into the thread. The invitation appears in the sidebar and in ⌘K only when a thread is ripe. Turn it on in Settings → Labs.

Changed

  • The writing surface is a touch warmer in light mode, and the editor column is slightly wider.

Fixed

  • Formatting a multi-paragraph selection now works. Bold, italic, highlight, strikethrough, and code applied across several paragraphs or list items now format each line correctly — matching how other editors handle it — instead of leaving stray markers, and a single undo reverts the whole thing. List bullets, checkboxes, and other line markers are left untouched.
  • Renaming a Region keeps its notes. Retitling a Region — by editing its heading, or renaming its file in Finder or iCloud — no longer quietly detaches the notes filed under it. A Region now remembers the tag it collects, so its members (and any you add later) stay put.
